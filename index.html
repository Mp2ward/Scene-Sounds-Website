The Problem

Scenarios
shouldn't be silent.

Every year, thousands of first aiders and emergency responders train to manage medical and traumatic emergencies. They learn the procedure. They learn the anatomy. But often they train in a classroom — quiet, calm, and nothing like the environments they will actually work in.

Under real conditions — sirens, wind, rain, explosions, screaming casualties, animals, radio traffic and more — the cognitive load spikes and fine motor skills degrade. Trainees who perform flawlessly in a classroom can often freeze or fumble in the field.

The Solution

One tap.
Full scene.

Scene Sounds is a purpose-built audio training platform. Instructors can use realistic layered soundscapes or pre-populated scenario sounds — RTC scenes, mass casualty incidents, battlefield chaos — and trainees perform their skills under genuine cognitive pressure.

It runs on any phone, tablet, or laptop. No hardware. No setup. One tap loads a full scenario. Individual sound layers are adjustable in real time so instructors can escalate or de-escalate the environment during the exercise.
